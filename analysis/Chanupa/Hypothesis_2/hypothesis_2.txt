HYPOTHESIS 2: GENDER DISPARITY IN SKILLED EMIGRANT COMPOSITION

Method: Paired comparison (year-wise) with assumption-based test selection
Sample Size (n): 32

1. HYPOTHESES:
   H0: There is no significant difference between female and male skilled emigration percentages.
   H1: Female skilled emigration percentage is significantly higher than male skilled emigration percentage.

2. ASSUMPTION CHECK:
   Shapiro-Wilk on paired differences (female - male): W = 0.913, p = 0.013
   IQR-based outliers in paired differences: 0

3. TEST RESULTS (Primary: Wilcoxon signed-rank, one-sided female > male):
   Female skilled mean (SD): 90.04% (5.38)
   Male skilled mean (SD): 59.27% (4.97)
   Mean paired difference (SD): 30.77 (8.83) percentage points
   W = 528.000, p < 0.001
   Effect size (Rank-biserial correlation): 1.000

4. CONCLUSION:
   Reject H0. Female skilled emigration share is statistically significantly higher than male share across years.
