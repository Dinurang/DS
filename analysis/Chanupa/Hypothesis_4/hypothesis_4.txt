HYPOTHESIS 4: POVERTY AS A PUSH FACTOR FOR EMIGRATION VOLUME

Method: Pearson product-moment correlation (full sample) with directional follow-up
Sample Size (n): 32

1. HYPOTHESES:
   H0: There is no significant linear correlation between poverty rate and emigration volume (rho = 0).
   H1: There is a significant linear correlation between poverty rate and emigration volume (rho != 0).

2. ASSUMPTION CHECK:
   Visual pattern check (time series + scatter): approximately linear inverse tendency with a retained shock-year outlier (2020).
   Complete-case observations used in test: no missing values in required columns after filtering.

3. TEST RESULTS (Primary: Pearson two-tailed; Supplementary: one-tailed rho < 0):
   Poverty rate vs emigration correlation (r): -0.359
   Test statistic: t = -2.10, df = 30
   Two-tailed p-value: 0.0438
   One-tailed p-value (rho < 0): 0.0219
   95% CI for r: [-0.629, -0.011]
   Coefficient of determination (R^2): 0.129

4. CONCLUSION:
   Reject H0. The two-tailed test indicates a statistically significant correlation between poverty rate and emigration volume.
