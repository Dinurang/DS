Gender Composition Transition and Parity Stability:

Analysis of the dataset from 1994 to 2025 shows a structural shift in the gender profile of Sri Lankan
labor migration. In the mid-90s, the flow was highly feminized, with females accounting for over 70% 
of total emigrants (e.g., 71.5% in 1994). This dominance was driven mainly by the skilled category, 
where 89.5% of females belonged. However, a steady transition toward a new gender equilibrium is observed
over the period. By the late 2010s, the male-to-female ratio inverted and stabilized near 65:35, reflecting 
a significant increase in male participation in the labor export market. Interestingly, while the 
overarching volume shifted toward males, the internal skill composition within genders remained remarkably 
static. Female migration continues to be dominated by the skilled sector (≈87% in 2017), whereas the male 
cohort maintains relatively more balanced split between skilled (≈61%) and low-skilled (≈39%) labor.
