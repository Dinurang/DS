GENDER COMPOSITION TRANSITION AND PARITY STABILITY 

Analysis of the dataset from 1994 to 2025 shows a structural shift in the gender profile of Sri Lankan 
labor migration. In the mid-90s, the flow was highly feminized, with females accounting for 72.8%
of total emigrants in 1994. This dominance was primarily driven by the skilled category, 
where 89.5% (1994) of females belonged.However, a steady transition toward a new gender equilibrium is observed 
over the period. The male-to-female ratio inverted significantly after 2008. While the male percentage reached 
a high of roughly 66% in the mid-2010s, it has stabilized near a 60:40 split in the most recent periods. 
Interestingly, while the overarching volume shifted toward males, the internal skill composition within 
genders remains distinct. Female migration continues to be dominated by the skilled sector 
(approximately 86% in 2017), whereas the male cohort maintains a more divided split, though shifting 
increasingly toward skilled labor (approximately 61% skilled vs 39% low-skilled in 2017).
