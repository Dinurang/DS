HYPOTHESIS TEST: Association Between Emigrant Age and Contract Duration

Method: Pearson Correlation Coefficient (r)

Sample Size (n): 32


HYPOTHESES:
   H0: 𝜌 = 0 There is no linear correlation between age and contract duration
   H1: 𝜌 > 0 There is a positive linear correlation 



=== Correlation Results for Hypothesis 3 ===

Variables: 'average_age_of_emigrant' vs 'average_contract_years'
Observations (n): 32
Degrees of Freedom (df): 30

Pearson Correlation Coefficient (r): 0.591
t-statistic: 4.01
p-value (one-sided): 0.0002
95% Confidence Interval for r: [0.305, 0.779]

Moderate-to-Strong Positive Correlation: 
The Pearson correlation coefficient (r) of 0.591 confirms a clear positive relationship. 
As the average age of emigrants goes up, the average contract duration also goes up.

High Statistical Significance: Our 𝑡-statistic is 4.01 and the 𝑝-value is 0.0002 
(which is well below the 0.001 threshold). This provides very strong evidence to reject the Null Hypothesis
 (H0).
 
Confidence Interval: The 95% confidence interval of [0.305, 0.779] is entirely above zero, 
further cementing the positive nature of this relationship.


CONCLUSION:
   Reject H0. There is a significant moderate positive correlation between age and contract duration.
