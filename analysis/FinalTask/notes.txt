Hypothesis 1: Trend in Gender Composition of Emigration
We investigated whether the proportion of female emigrants has
experienced a statistically significant decline over the last three
decades. A simple linear regression model was employed with Year
as the independent variable and female_perc (percentage of female
emigrants) as the dependent variable.
• Null Hypothesis (𝐻0): There is no linear trend in the female
emigration proportion (𝛽 = 0).
• Alternative Hypothesis (𝐻1): There is a significant down-
ward linear trend (𝛽 < 0).
Results: The regression analysis revealed a strong negative re-
lationship between time and the proportion of female emigrants.
The model yielded a slope coefficient of 𝛽 = −1.271 (𝑆𝐸 = 0.116),
indicating that the female share of total emigration decreased by
approximately 1.27 percentage points per year. The 𝑡-statistic was
−10.96 (𝑑 𝑓 = 30) with 𝑝-value < 0.001. The 95% confidence interval
for the slope was [−1.507, −1.035] with coefficient of determination
𝑅2 = 0.80.
Interpretation: The extremely low 𝑝-value provides compelling
evidence to reject the null hypothesis. The results confirm a statis-
tically significant and steady long-term decline in the proportion
of female emigrants, shifting from a female-dominated migra-
tion pattern in the early 1990s to a male-dominated one in the 2020s.
