================================================================================
DESCRIPTIVE FINDINGS
================================================================================

  1)STRUCTURAL BREAK — 2022 Economic Crisis:
  The 2022–2023 period represents the most significant structural break in the
  entire 32-year dataset. The rupee devaluation of +62.20% in 2022 resulted in
  4 consecutive IQR outlier years (2022–2025) in the exchange rate series. This
  event is not a statistical anomaly — it is an extreme but real macroeconomic
  event and should be retained in all analyses.

  2)COVID-19 — 2020 Emigration Outlier:
  The 2020 emigration figure (53,711 workers) is a genuine IQR lower-fence
  outlier. It represents a 73.55% year-over-year decline — the sharpest in the
  dataset — and reflects the near-complete cessation of international labour
  migration due to global COVID-19 travel restrictions. Again, this is a real
  event and should not be treated as erroneous data.

  3)REMITTANCES AS AN ECONOMIC LIFELINE:
  Sri Lanka's personal remittances grew from USD 715 Mn (1994) to USD 8,076 Mn
  (2025), representing an 11.3x increase over 32 years. The 2020 COVID year is
  particularly notable: despite a 73.55% collapse in emigration, remittances
  rose 5.73% in 2020 as the existing diaspora sent more money home to support
  families affected by the pandemic. This demonstrates the counter-cyclical
  nature of remittance flows relative to acute shocks.

  4)GENDERED STRUCTURAL SHIFT:
  A fundamental shift in the gender composition of emigration occurred between
  the Civil War era (female-dominated, avg 64.6%) and the Post-War era (male-
  dominated, avg 58.6%), driven by Gulf construction booms and policy changes
  restricting female domestic worker migration. This shift has implications for
  household income composition and vulnerability.

  5)INCREASING CONTRACT DURATION — DESPERATION SIGNAL:
  The average contract duration has increased monotonically from 1.8 years (1994)
  to 3.5 years (2025) — a 94.4% increase. The near-perfect correlation with the
  dollar rate (r = 0.951) suggests that rupee weakness is associated with Sri
  Lankan workers committing to longer overseas contracts, consistent with economic
  constraint driving longer-term migration commitments.

  6)OIL PRICE CYCLES AND GULF LABOUR DEMAND:
  Gulf Cooperation Council countries account for the majority of Sri Lankan
  emigrant destinations. Brent crude oil prices serve as a reliable proxy for
  Gulf economic activity and therefore Gulf hiring capacity. The cyclical nature
  of oil prices (no distributional outliers; W = 0.936, p = 0.056, near-normal)
  contrasts with the trend-dominated nature of emigration, remittances, and the
  exchange rate — making it the most exogenous variable in the system.
