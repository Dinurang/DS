This data combines records from the Sri Lanka Bureau of Foreign Employment (SLBFE),
 Central Bank of Sri Lanka (CBSL), and recent 2024 Census insights.

📚 Primary References
Sri Lanka Bureau of Foreign Employment (SLBFE) - Annual Statistical Reports (2000–2024):
Data Used: Registration by manpower level (Skilled/Low-skilled), Gender-wise departures, and District-wise registration tables.
Specific Table Refs: Table 1 (Registration by Gender), Table 3/3A (Manpower Level 1994–Present), and Table 1.13 (District Distribution).
Note: 2024 figures are based on the SLBFE's 29th series report (released late 2025).
Central Bank of Sri Lanka (CBSL) - Annual Reports & Quarterly Bulletins:
Data Used: Worker remittance figures and total departure estimates.
Specific Ref: "Quarterly Bulletin of Workers' Remittances and Labour Migration" (Issues for Q1–Q4 2024 and 2025).
Department of Census and Statistics (DCS) - Sri Lanka Labour Force Survey:
Data Used: Age profile distributions (Age 15–29, 30–39, etc.) and educational attainment levels.

🔍 Specific Data Verification Notes
Average Age (2022–2025): These figures reflect the "Youth Exodus" documented in the 2024 SLBFE Chairperson's Year-End Review, which noted that the modal age group for departures shifted from 35–39 down to 25–29 during the economic crisis.
Average Stay (Years): While standard Middle East contracts remain 2 years, the mean stay increased because of the surge in SSW (Specified Skilled Worker) visas for Japan and E-9 visas for South Korea, which allow stays of 3 to 5+ years.
District Percentages: These are calculated by dividing the SLBFE "Registration by District" count by the "Total Registration" for each specific year.
